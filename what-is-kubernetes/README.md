## What is Kubernetes?

[Kubernetes](https://kubernetes.io/) is an open-source platform for automating the deployment, scaling, and management of containerized applications. Originally developed by **Google** and later open-sourced in 2014, Kubernetes has become a foundational tool in cloud-native computing. It is written in the [Go programming language](https://golang.org/).

Kubernetes is designed to streamline the orchestration of complex applications running in containers, especially across multiple servers in enterprise environments.

## Name and Origin

The name "Kubernetes" comes from the Greek word **κυβερνήτης** (pronounced ky-ver-NEE-tees), meaning _"helmsman"_ or _"pilot."_ This reflects its role as a guide for managing and orchestrating containers. 

> Kubernetes is often abbreviated as **K8s** (with "8" standing for the eight letters between "K" and "S")

Kubernetes evolved from Google’s earlier container orchestration system, **Borg**. Insights from Borg heavily influenced Kubernetes, giving it the tools and flexibility needed for today’s complex, distributed applications.

## Benefits

Kubernetes is designed to solve a lot of the problems that come with running containerized workloads in large enterprise environments. These problems include:

- Provisioning – Setting up containers on demand as needed.
- Deployment – Easily deploying applications with rollouts and rollbacks.
- Scaling – Adjusting resources dynamically based on demand.
- Networking – Facilitating secure communication between containers.
- Load Balancing – Distributing traffic to avoid overloading containers.
- Cluster Health – Monitoring and ensuring the reliability of applications and resources.

With Kubernetes, development and operations teams can focus on building and optimizing applications, while Kubernetes handles underlying infrastructure tasks.

> Kubernetes abstracts the complexities of managing individual servers, giving teams a unified platform for running applications anywhere in the cluster.

## How to Pronounce "Kubernetes"

Pronunciation of Kubernetes is often a controversial topic and many people have differing opinions.

The most common pronunciation is:

- _koo-ber-NET-ees_ ✅

Other pronunciations like:

- _koo-ber-NEAT-ees_ ❌
- _koo-ber-NEATS_ ❌

are less common and not standard.
